üó∫Ô∏è The Blueprint

To build a dynamic story, we need to follow two main steps:

  1. Gather Intelligence: Ask your users to list a bunch of information about themselves: things they like, things they hate, names of family and friends... keep it wacky!
  2. Weave the Tale: Construct your story using the variables you created. Remember concatenation and the use of commas , and quotes "".
Tip: Make sure to only work one paragraph at a time. Otherwise, things could get a bit messy! Using print() with nothing inside can help add blank lines for spacing.

üíª Syntax: Building the Simulator

Here is the code structure we are aiming for today. Delete any old code in main.py and try this:

print("Welcome to your Adventure Story Simulator.")
print()
print("I am going to ask you a bunch of questions and then create an epic story with you as the star.")
print()

name = input("What is your name? ")
enemyName = input("What is your enemy's name? ")
superPower = input("What is your super power? ")

print()
print("Hello", name, "Your ability to", superPower, "will make sure you never have to look at", enemyName, "again!")

Code Analysis:

  • Empty Print: print() creates a blank line to separate text.
  • Variables: We capture user answers into descriptive variables like enemyName.
  • Concatenation: We use commas inside the final print() to stitch the text and variables together seamlessly.
